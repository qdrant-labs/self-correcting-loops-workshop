Takeaways

  • The fusion lift shows up in AUC but mostly vanishes at a real operating point. LR(dense_variance + score_variance) is the best model on ranking quality (test AUC 0.729 → 0.736, PR-AUC 0.744 → 0.775). But at matched recall (section 3b) it ties the single signal: at ~80% catch rate both waste 52.9% of strong retrieval (53.2% for the single signal). The AUC gain (+0.007) is also inside the 5-fold CV spread (±0.0315). Net: marginal and corpus-specific, not a step change.
  • The cheapest win here is not the classifier at all. The live OR-gate catches 82.2% of weak retrieval at 51.8% FPR; a single well-set dense_variance threshold hits the same catch rate at 53.2% FPR. Firing when EITHER signal trips over-escalates - tightening the gate to one calibrated signal saves wasted cost with no model at all. Worth checking independently of this study.
  • More signals and fancier models do NOT help - they overfit. Adding all 19 features or switching to gradient boosting lowers test AUC and blows the cal→test gap wide open (GBT: cal 0.893 vs test 0.751 on 657 calibration rows). On data this size, two regularised signals beat nineteen.
  • Fusion is a weighted compromise across query types. On validation (single-hop / multi-hop AUC): dense_variance 0.848 / 0.598; score_variance 0.796 / 0.652; the fused LR 0.868 / 0.617. The fused gate tracks the stronger signal on each type but is pulled toward the majority class, so a type-specific signal can still beat it on the minority slice. If one query type is what you care about, its dedicated signal may still win there.
  • The strongest single signal overall is still dense_variance. None of the 12 engineered signals top it on overall validation AUC. The most interesting of the new ones is divergence_rbo_bm25, the strongest engineered signal on the multi-hop slice (val multi-hop AUC 0.643) - a candidate for the "test on your corpus" catalog, not the notebook spine.
  • Interpretability is preserved. The winning gate is two readable weights (dense_variance -0.7544, score_variance -0.2195; both negative = low value flags weak retrieval). Adopting it would not turn the workshop into an ML-modelling lesson.
  • This measures DETECTION only. Whether the marginal AUC/PR-AUC lift actually moves the end-to-end cost/quality frontier needs the classifier dropped into the live gate and a headline re-run. Given the size of the lift here, do that before committing to it in the notebook.
